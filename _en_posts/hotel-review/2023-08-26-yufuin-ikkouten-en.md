Day 2 in Yufuin.

After breakfast kaiseki at our first ryokan, we checked out and headed into town.



---



## Morning Kaiseki



![Morning Kaiseki](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0405.webp)



Lighter than the previous evening's spread, but still delicious. Natto, tamago kake gohan (TKG), grilled salmon, salad, and a warm soup.



![Tamago Kake Gohan](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0409.webp)



![Egg Yolk](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0414.webp)



Look at that yolk. I wanted it so badly I actually bought Japanese eggs and soy sauce on my next trip back — still couldn't recreate it. The rice makes all the difference.



---



## Milch — Soufflé Cheesecake



![Milch Exterior](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0430.webp)



Walking toward Kinrin Lake, I spotted freshly baked cheesecake in a shop window and had to stop. Turns out Milch is a well-known local bakery.



![Soufflé Cheesecake](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0432.webp)



¥980 per slice. Worth every yen.

When ordering, you choose cold or hot — always go **cold**. I liked it so much I tried making it at home twice. It's harder than it looks.



---



## Walking to Kinrin Lake



![Yufuin Shopping Street](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0438.webp)



![Shopping Street 2](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0439.webp)



The streets between town and Kinrin Lake are lined with all kinds of shops. Anime merch, snacks, souvenirs — the prices are steep but the browsing is free.



![Kinsho Croquette](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0453.webp)



My sister recommended the famous Kinsho Croquette. She was right — absolutely delicious. Don't skip it.



---



## Kinrin Lake



![Kinrin Lake](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0454.webp)



![Kinrin Lake 2](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0470.webp)



Yufuin's iconic lake. Great for a leisurely stroll — I'd only visited in winter before, but the atmosphere is something else regardless of season.



![Kinrin Lake 3](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0475.webp)



![Yufuin Alley](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0496.webp)



On the way back toward the station — a very Japanese alleyway that I had to stop and photograph. Also: don't leave Japan without hitting a Lawson convenience store.



---



## Ikkouten Ryokan



![Ikkouten Exterior](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0498.webp)



We took a taxi from Yufuin Station to our second ryokan — Ikkouten. Mount Yufu with its snow-capped peak is visible in the distance.



![Deer](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0503.webp)



The hillside behind the property was full of deer. They're extremely wary of people, but I noticed their tails are shaped like little white hearts — had to get a shot.



---



## Private Villa Interior



![Villa Interior](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0516.webp)



Six of us stayed together, so we booked the full private villa. Walking in, my first reaction was just: "wow." 2.5 floors, spacious, beautifully maintained.



![Second Floor](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0519.webp)



Six beds across the main floor, upper floor, and a loft-style top level.



![Welcome Sweets](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0526.webp)



Welcome sweets on the table — the wagashi-style confection was surprisingly good.



![Sunset View](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0527.webp)



![Dusk](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0529.webp)



The property at sunset.



---



## Open-Air Onsen



![Open-Air Onsen](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_2/IMG_0540.webp)



The villa has its own private bath, but the real highlight is this open-air onsen.

Soaking under the stars while the mountain air drifts in — that's Yufuin at its best. Every time I come here, it feels like something special. Can't wait to come back.
