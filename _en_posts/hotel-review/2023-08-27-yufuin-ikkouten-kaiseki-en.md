After settling in at Ikkouten, it was time for the evening kaiseki dinner.

The restaurant is a short 5-minute walk from the guest rooms — a separate building altogether.



---



## Ikkouten — Kaiseki Dinner



![Kaiseki Dining Room](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_3/06473B61-A98A-44BA-BAB6-1A36C68A4BFE_4_5005_c.webp)



Our server greeted us warmly at the entrance and led us to a private room visible through the window. Blankets were already waiting on the seats.



![Dinner Course Menu](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_3/0B871F1B-12DB-4B43-B3C3-7D6D945B9B16_4_5005_c.webp)



The course menu on the table. The lineup: appetizer → soup → sashimi (tuna, red snapper, squid) → grilled shrimp with yuzu-miso → wagyu steak → local chicken broth → rice, miso soup, pickled vegetables → dessert.



![Appetizer](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_3/2FE1879D-38B6-47E1-A992-054265BACBAE_4_5005_c.webp)



The table was already set with several small dishes when we arrived. The opening spread featured anchovy and sea urchin preparations — bold, briny flavors. A bit salty for my taste, but well made.



![Sashimi](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_3/1E708D53-A54F-468D-9FB3-58E00D7BBE2A_1_102_o.webp)



And then the sashimi arrived. Tuna, red snapper, squid — the tuna especially was incredible. Just melts.



![Wagyu Steak](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_3/2A23D494-FB5F-4A57-92CE-625292829E0F_1_105_c.webp)



From the sea to the land — wagyu steak, cooked to a perfect medium with light seasoning. Also melts. That's the theme of this dinner.



![Grilled Shrimp & Dessert](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_3/615C375C-2916-4231-BC31-CE40A20B7E3C_1_102_o.webp)



Grilled shrimp with yuzu-miso glaze, and fresh fruit for dessert. A genuinely excellent dinner from start to finish.

## Villa & Minibar



![Villa Room](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_3/9C29524E-59EA-42A6-ACE6-62EB4FD5BF5F_1_105_c.webp)



![Minibar](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_3/1FB52AAF-2C2F-4190-AE51-06AEC5893B7E_1_105_c.webp)



Clean, well-equipped, and notably — the minibar was completely free. Tea, six beers, yogurt, capsule coffee refills. All complimentary. Not something you see often at this level.



![Villa Exterior](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_3/A109845D-A6A8-46B6-816F-B5B40D1A7075_1_102_o.webp)

## Yufuin Burger & Heading Home



![Yufuin Burger](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_3/BD68CC4C-0DA5-4A82-9158-3F0243A0D5C2_1_102_o.webp)



After checkout we walked around town. Stopped at a place called Yufuin Burger — the patty was massive, had to cut it in half. Absolutely worth it.



![Airport](/assets/images/hotel-review/fuk_3/FA41CB28-EEBD-415D-8463-C838327F987A_4_5005_c.webp)



(Side note: I found out afterward that I'd had COVID the entire trip. Somehow my taste and smell stayed intact, so I experienced everything fully — which is either lucky or deeply unfair to everyone around me.)

First Japan trip in a while. No regrets, loved every bit of it. Though there were *a lot* of Korean tourists everywhere — you couldn't walk a block without hearing Korean.

Japan's best feature is still the ryokan. Already looking forward to the next one.
